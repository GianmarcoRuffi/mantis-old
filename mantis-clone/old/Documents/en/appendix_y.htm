Y-3      Demo Team Program Mission

 

To develop, support, and train a group of skilled Demo Team members that are motivated to help fellow players sample new UDE games.

 

 

Y-4      Becoming a Member

 

To become a UDE Demo Team member, you must be at least 18 years of age and pass the level 1 Demo Team specialization exam. The exam is available online at www.ude.com/op/certprogram.aspx

 

A score of 90% is required to pass the test. Once the test is passed, you will receive an email confirming that you have achieved level 1 in the Demo Team specialization. At that point, you are officially a UDE Demo Team member!

 

Demo Team members will be eligible to receive a starter or demo deck of most products that UDE releases.

 

 

Y-5.   New Product Mailings and Information

 

The first Demo Team mailing will be sent out between May and June 2006. This mailing will contain product and materials that should be used to demo UDE games. After the initial mailing, there will be two mailings of new product per year to Demo Team members who have been active in the program.

 

For most product releases, we will ship starter decks to Demo Team members so they can generate excitement and buzz for the game as soon as it is on the shelves.

 

Demo Team members may be given advance information about upcoming products and releases, and also given a chance to receive demo materials before the product hits shelves.

 

 

Y-6      Specialization Level Requirements

 

            Level 1

·        Pass the level 1 online Demo Team specialization exam

 

Level 2

·        Pass the level 2 written Demo Team specialization exam and interview

·        Successfully administer and report at least 30 demos

·        Earn level 1 certification in two other UDE specializations

·        Have recommendation from a store owner or level 3 Demo Team member

 

Level 3

·        Pass the level 3 written Demo Team specialization exam and interview

·        Successfully administer and report at least 100 demos

·        Earn level 1 certification in player management

·        Earn level 2 certification in any other UDE specialization

·        Have at least 1 year experience as UDE Demo Team member

 

Level 4

·        Pass the level 4 written Demo Team specialization exam and interview

·        Successfully administer and report at least 400 demos

·        Earn level 2 certification in player management

·        Earn level 3 certification in any other UDE specialization

·        Have at least 2 years experience as UDE Demo Team member

 

Level 5

·        Pass the level 4 written Demo Team specialization exam and interview

·        Successfully administer and report at least 1000 demos

·        Earn level 3 certification in player management

·        Earn level 4 certification in any other UDE specialization

·        Have at least 3 years experience as UDE Demo Team member

 

 

 

Y-7      Demo Team Member Responsibilities

           

Demo team members often have the opportunity to meet new players in many locations. It's important that they:

 

·        Act in a professional and respectful manner at all times

·        Learn the basic rules of any game being demoed

·        Avoid making negative comments about competitor products

·        Avoid making negative comments about UDE products

·        Bring all materials to a demo location, and a minimum of 2 demo decks for each game being shown.

·        Notify location management before administering a demo

·        Perform demos in a comprehensive and understandable manner

·        Clean up the area after performing a demo

·        Accurately report all demo activities within 1 week of a demo

·        Wear clean and professional clothing when administering a demo (a UDE logo shirt is preferred)

·        Complete the demo is a timely fashion, especially if other people are waiting for the next demo

·        During a demo, ensure the demo recipient has a positive experience and understands the game after the demo is complete

 

 

Y-8      Demo Team Leader Responsibilities—How do I become one? How are we tracking these people?

 

Demo team leaders are experienced Demo Team members who have been appointed by Upper Deck to be liaisons for their state, region, or province. It is important that they fulfill all of the Demo Team member responsibilities in addition to the following:

 

·        Maintain an organized and detailed list of all Demo Team members in their area

·        Maintain and frequently check an email address that may be given out to all Demo Team members in their area

·        Help train Demo Team members

·        Help to ensure all active Demo Team members in their area have the tools needed to effectively perform demos

·        Visit hobby stores in their designated areas to inform them of the UDE demo services available and put them in contact with a local Demo Team member

·        Attend the annual Demo Team Conference and represent their area by providing feedback and suggestions to improve the program.

·        Pass the level 1 exam for each game that is available

·        Contact hobby stores and perform customer service calls to verify that the Demo Team members in their area are performing their duties as required

 

 

Y-9      Rewards Mailing

           

As with the UDE judge rewards programs, all Demo Team rewards will be mailed twice per year, typically in May and November. Demo team members should allow six to eight weeks to receive rewards.

 

Rewards will be similar to the judge rewards strategy. Details of rewards will be available at www.ude.com/demo.

 

 

Y-10    Demo Auditing

 

Periodically, the Demo Team Manager will conduct audits of demos to confirm Demo Team reports. In the unlikely event that fraudulent reports are discovered from a Demo Team member, immediate suspension from all UDE activity would result.

 

Degrading UDE, our products or staff members, or any of our competitors and their products and staff will result in immediate suspension from all UDE activity, and removal from the Demo Team program.

 

UDE reserves the right to remove members from the program at any time for any reason.

 

 

Y-11    Demo Reporting

 

Event reports are often used in order to track a Demo Team member’s progress in the program. Event reports should be submitted via the online reporting form located at www.ude.com/demo within 7 days of running a demo event. Any events submitted after the 7 day window will not be credited towards your reward mailing.

 

Typically you will submit one event report for each demo session that you performed. However, when at a convention or demo day, please submit one report with the total number of demos performed and people demoed to that day, and mark in the “comments” field the name of the convention/demo day.

 

There is no limit to the number of demos that can be submitted at a time, but Demo Team members must submit at least one demo report per month in order to stay active in the program.

 

 

Y-12    UDE Forum Guidelines

 

UDE maintains a forum at www.ude.com/op/community for Demo Team members to exchange ideas and information. This forum is also used to communicate upcoming demo opportunities at tournament stores, conventions, etc.

 

Demo team members should ensure their posts are on the topic of demos and are professional at all times. Demo team members should not ask game rules or tournament policy questions on this forum, but should direct them to the game-appropriate forums found at www.ude.com/op/community.

 

Flaming is not tolerated, and flaming of any person, policy, tournament, etc., is grounds for immediate removal from the Demo Team program.
